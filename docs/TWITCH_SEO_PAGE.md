### 3. Types of Twitch Sponsorships

Four distinct sponsorship formats with pricing multipliers:

#### Quick Mention (30-60 seconds)
- **Pricing**: 30-50% of base rate
- **Example**: Base $1,000 → charge $300-$500
- **Best for**: One-off deals, testing partnerships
- **Description**: Brief shoutout, logo display, or prepared message

#### Dedicated Segment (15-30 minutes)
- **Pricing**: 100% of base rate (standard)
- **Example**: Base $1,000 → charge $1,000
- **Best for**: Gaming sponsors, hardware reviews, software demos
- **Description**: Focused gameplay, review, or hands-on demonstration

#### Full Sponsored Stream (2-4 hours)
- **Pricing**: 2.0-3.0x base rate
- **Example**: Base $1,000 → charge $2,000-$3,000
- **Best for**: Game launches, major product releases
- **Description**: Entire stream dedicated to the sponsor

#### Multi-Stream Series
- **Pricing**: 4.0x+ base rate (with 10-20% volume discount)
- **Example**: Base $1,000 → charge $3,500-$4,000 for 4 streams
- **Best for**: Long-term partnerships, ongoing games/services
- **Description**: Package deal across multiple sessions

### 4. Why Twitch Rates Differ from YouTube

Explains four key differences:

1. **Live Interaction = Authentic Engagement**
   - Unedited, genuine reactions
   - Higher trust but riskier for brands
   - No multiple takes or heavy scripting

2. **No Replay Value = Lower Total Reach**
   - YouTube videos accumulate views over time
   - Twitch streams reach only concurrent viewers
   - VODs get 5-10% of live viewership
   - Justifies lower CPM vs YouTube

3. **Followers ≠ Viewers**
   - Many followers are inactive
   - Brands pay for concurrent viewers, not follower count
   - CCV is the only metric that matters

4. **Subs and Donations Signal Real Value**
   - Built-in monetization proves audience spending
   - High sub count can justify 20-40% higher rates
   - Shows audience doesn't just watch—they pay

### 5. Rate-Increasing Factors

#### Content Niche (with CPM ranges)
- **Tech & Hardware Reviews**: $20-40 CPM (highest)
- **Just Chatting (business/finance)**: $15-30 CPM
- **Competitive Gaming (Valorant, CS:GO, LoL)**: $12-25 CPM
- **Variety Gaming**: $10-20 CPM
- **Music/Creative**: $8-15 CPM
- **Casual/Party Games**: $8-15 CPM (lowest)

#### Chat Engagement
- Active chat = higher value than silent viewers
- Brands can see chat activity = social proof
- Lively chat means sponsor message gets noticed

#### Subscriber Count
- High sub-to-viewer ratio proves loyalty
- 40% sub rate = exceptional (can add 30-50% to base rate)
- Proves audience willing to spend money

#### Audience Demographics
- **Age**: 18-34 male most valuable (disposable income)
- **Location**: US/UK/Canada worth 3-5x global average
- **Spending power**: PC gamers > console gamers

#### Exclusivity & Duration
- 30-day exclusivity = 30-50% premium
- 3-6 month deals = premium pricing
- Guaranteeing sustained exposure = higher rates

### 6. FAQ Section (6 questions)

#### Q1: How do I calculate my Twitch sponsorship rate?
**Answer**: Simple formula = $1-$2 per concurrent viewer per stream. Adjust based on niche, engagement, and sponsorship type. Use calculator for precision.

#### Q2: Should I charge per hour or per stream?
**Answer**: **Per stream is industry standard.** Flat rate for 2-4 hour stream protects both parties. Exception: 8+ hour streams may need hourly or split into two.

#### Q3: Do subscribers matter more than viewers?
**Answer**: **Viewers are quantity, subs are quality signal.** Brands pay for reach (CCV), but high sub ratio proves engagement. Use it to negotiate up.

#### Q4: How much do small Twitch streamers make from sponsors?
**Answer**: 50-200 CCV streamers typically make $200-$1,000 per sponsored stream. 1-2 sponsors per month = $400-$2,000 extra income. Consistency is key.

#### Q5: What's the difference between a sponsor and an affiliate deal?
**Answer**: 
- **Sponsorship**: Flat fee upfront ($X to promote)
- **Affiliate**: Commission on sales (5-20% per purchase)
- **Best**: Hybrid deals ($500 flat + 10% commission)

#### Q6: How do I find sponsors for my Twitch channel?
**Answer**: Strategies:
- Join gaming networks (PowerSpike, Ader, Matchmade)
- Apply to affiliate programs (GFuel, DXRacer)
- Cold outreach to brands you use
- Network with other streamers
- Create a media kit
Start with smaller brands, build portfolio, then approach bigger sponsors.

### 7. Common Mistakes

Four critical mistakes streamers make:

1. **Quoting Follower Count Instead of CCV**
   - "I have 20K followers" means nothing if CCV is only 100
   - Always lead with concurrent viewers

2. **Accepting Free Product as Payment**
   - A $300 gaming chair ≠ 3-hour sponsored stream
   - Accept free product only if: buying it anyway, comes with cash, or under 50 CCV building portfolio

3. **Not Setting Boundaries**
   - Sponsors will ask for everything (stream, clips, YouTube, Twitter)
   - Contract should state: "One 3-hour stream, period"
   - Additional content = separate negotiation

4. **Promoting Products You Haven't Tested**
   - Your community trusts you
   - Don't promote scams for quick money
   - Only promote products you've used or believe in
